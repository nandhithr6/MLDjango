Breast Cancer Prediction Model

Welcome to the Breast Cancer Prediction system! This tool predicts whether a breast tumor is benign or malignant using machine learning algorithms.

Model Accuracy

The model achieves an accuracy of 96% based on the dataset, making it reliable for real-time cancer detection. It was trained using a random classifier, which, despite its simplicity, provides a strong baseline. This high accuracy suggests that the model captures important patterns in the data, and can be further improved with more advanced algorithms.

Dataset Description

The dataset used for training the model is the famous Breast Cancer Wisconsin (Diagnostic) dataset. It contains 569 instances with 30 features describing characteristics of cell nuclei present in breast cancer biopsies. The features include:

  • Radius (mean of distances from center to points on the perimeter)
  • Texture (standard deviation of gray-scale values)
  • Perimeter (length of the tumor's perimeter)
  • Area (size of the tumor area)
  • Concavity, smoothness, compactness, and other morphological properties of the tumor cells.

The target variable is a classification label where 0 denotes a benign tumor and 1 denotes a malignant tumor.
